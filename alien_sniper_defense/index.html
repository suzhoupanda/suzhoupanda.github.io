Alien Sniper Defense

App Information

Record Gameplay (available in previous versions)
Record gameplay to share with friends on social media or post on personal blogs. Recording is automatically enabled upon app launch. Recording can also enabled or disabled during gameplay by pressing by pause button at the lower left corner of the screen and then pressing the "Record Gameplay" button on the UI menu that follows. (Note: The app MUST be in record mode in order for background music and audio to be enabled. Recording is stopped automatically upon winning or losing a level. Recorded gameplay clips can be viewed from the level selection menu interface by pressing "View Recorded Gameplay" in the bottom toolbar)
Localization for Chinese(simplified and traditional)
This app is available in a Chinese version (both simplified and traditional characters). To switch to the chinese version, change the language settings of the app to Chinese. There are number of variants for Chinese localization, but the simplified (mainland China) and traditional(Taiwan/Hong Kong) settings should suffice.
How is the crosshair used to target and shoot enemies?

First touch down on the crosshair, then drag the crosshair to the enemy's position. Once the crosshair has been moved so as to overlap or cover the enemy, tap down on the crosshair to fire a laser. Some enemies may require multiple hits in order to be killed.

Player Movement/Enemies Help Topics
How is the game paused or restarted?

The game is automatically paused when the app is minimized or enters the background. You can pause the game by tapping the small, round menu icon in the lower left corner of the screen. Once tapped, two buttons will appear: one which allows you to restart the current level, and another for returning to the main transition menu.

Changing Game State Help Topics
What are the game rules? How does the player win or lose?

For minimum kills mode, the player wins when the minimum number of on-screen enemies have been killed, as indicated in the scoreboard. If the number of on-screen enemies exceeds a threshold value, the player loses. Therefore, the player must kill enemies faster than they can spawn on the screen. For time limit mode, the principle is the same. A minimum number of enemies must be killed before the time limit expires. If the minimum kills is not reached by the time the time limit is reached, the player loses and must restart the level.

Game Rules Help Topics
What are the differences between hard, medium, and difficult mode?

The difficulty level correlates with a number of parameters that make it more difficult for the player to win. The difficult setting therefore means enemies spawn at shorter intervals and more enemies spawn at each spawning interval. In addition, for some enemies, a more difficult setting may mean faster or more randomized movement or behavior.

Video Previews

  • Wingman

    Wingman seem to flap in a single position for a short period of time but will immediately hide behind a random cloud at regular intervals. Once hidden behind a cloud, they cannot be hit. Wingman are quite evasive and enjoy hiding behind clouds, appearing and hiding in a way that is frustrating for even the most skilled snipers.

  • Stealth Ships

    Stealth ships enter an invisible "stealth" mode at regular intervals, and will also change the direction of their flight path. While in stealth mode, they are invulnerable to attack. Once they are visible to the player, they can be hit. Stealth ships require multiple hits before they can be destroyed, and will give off flames when damaged.

  • UFOreos

    UFOreos move about randomly, colliding with each other and bouncing off the edges of the screen. They spawn in larger numbers and can be easy to kill when spawning in large batches. The downside is that if too many are spawned, the player loses. When hit, a UFOreos will turn red and fall off the screen.

  • Hover Borgs

    Hover borgs can change between manned and unmanned mode. In unmanned mode, they are invulnerable to attack. Only when they are manned, can they be hit. Furthemore, each hover borg requires multiple hits before it can be destroyed. In some game versions, the background clouds can be converted to vortex fields, which are triggered by shooting at a given background cloud. When the vortex field is triggered, all of the hover borgs present will be drawn in, making it easier to target them.

  • Fluorescent Bats

    These bats can be hard to see in the dark reaches of outer space. Fortunately, your crosshair has a lightnode specially designed for hunting these nefarious bats. As long as your crosshair manages to get within a close enough range, nearby bats should become illuminated and therefore easier to target. The bats vary in size, so smaller ones may be harder to see and may require more active movement of the crosshair.
